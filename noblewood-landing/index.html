About Noblewood

Noblewood of Utica is a new neighborhood development in Utica, Indiana

Situated in historic Utica, Indiana, and just minutes from Louisville, our lush private lots are soon to be the site of an exclusive new riverside community. Inspired by Utica’s long and rich history, Noblewood offers the advantages of a quaint, peaceful community with the amenities of the city just a short drive away. In addition to our scenic lots and impressive wooded views, Utica’s enduring historic charm makes Noblewood a beautiful and welcoming place to call home.

Noblewood at Utica features 87 exclusive lots, consisting of 63 single-family lots and 24 duplex lots. Our single-family lots start at $119,900, with an average home price of $575,000, while our patio homes average at a price of $525,000. If you are interested in a more secluded, spacious property, Noblewood offers 26 impressive 1-acre lots with an average home price of $750,000. All Noblewood homes feature the same custom construction that exists in our developments in East Louisville, so you can design a beautiful dream home that suits your lifestyle.

OUR HISTORY

Our legacy Dates Back centuries

The town of Utica, Indiana, dates back centuries, with the first settlement founded in 1794. Noblewood at Utica is named after one of the town’s founding fathers, James Noble Wood, who established a ferry system that allowed passengers to safely travel across the Ohio River to Harrods Creek, Kentucky, while avoiding the dangerous Falls of the Ohio. This ferry system turned Utica into a prosperous, bustling town and established it as an important crossing point between Kentucky and Indiana.

To this day, Utica’s rich history remains an important feature of the town. You can still find historic log cabins, scenic limestone quarries, and other notable landmarks that honor the heritage of the town. In addition, the thriving local businesses and organizations help to make Utica a friendly and welcoming community. With the construction of the new East End Bridge, Utica now serves as the first exit off the bridge from Prospect, Kentucky. The town has again become a flourishing connection point between our two states, while still fostering the peaceful community and slower pace of life that Utica’s residents value.
